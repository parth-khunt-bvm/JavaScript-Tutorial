JavaScript Tutorial - const, let and var

In JavaScript, const, let, and var are used to declare variables, but they have some differences in terms of scope, hoisting, and mutability

  • var
    • Variables declared with var are function-scoped or globally scoped.
    • They are hoisted to the top of their function or global scope and initialized with undefined.
    • You can redeclare a variable using var within the same scope, and it won't throw an error.
    • They have function-level scope, meaning they are accessible anywhere within the function in which they are declared.
    • They can be reassigned and updated.
  • let
    • Variables declared with let are block-scoped (scoped to the nearest enclosing curly braces {}).
    • They are hoisted to the top of their block, but not initialized.
    • let variables are not redeclarable within the same scope. Attempting to redeclare a variable with let in the same block scope will result in a syntax error.
    • They have block-level scope, meaning they are accessible only within the block in which they are declared.
    • They can be reassigned and updated.
  • const
    • Variables declared with const are also block-scoped.
    • They are hoisted to the top of their block, but not initialized.
    • const variables must be initialized with a value at the time of declaration, and once initialized, their value cannot be changed.
    • They are not redeclarable within the same scope. Attempting to redeclare a variable with const in the same block scope will result in a syntax error.
    • They have block-level scope, meaning they are accessible only within the block in which they are declared.
